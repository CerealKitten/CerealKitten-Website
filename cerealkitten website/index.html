About Me

My name is Franco Paniagua, I prefer to go by Frank in person, and CerealKitten is my name for just about everything online, it's a bit of an inside joke. I'm currently a freelance designer and I especially like to be given creative liberty with visuals, I am particularily fond of hiding messages and codes in my designs. I have training in finance management thanks to the opportunity of being the treasurer for the design club at Eastern Washington University.

As a freelance designer I have had experience in web design as well asset creation, branding, and motion design. Marketing is a field I am wanting to do more work in, but I am particularily fond of working on illustration. What I enjoy most about this professional field is being able to learn new tricks and apply them in almost any project. I use the Adobe suite for any digital work, and Illustrator is my favorite program in the suite.

I think my biggest accomplishment in regards to working in design is the fact I have been working with the software for only a couple years, and I am already finding myself teaching my peers and sometimes my instructors new ways to use the tools the software provides. Teaching others new tricks is a nice way for me to check on myself and make sure I am always improving.

I am looking for new opportunities to apply my skills which include: illustration, design, branding, packaging design, video editing, motion design, asset creation, financial management, and creative writing among others.

Gallery

I've decided to divide the gallery into three sections according to how I categorize the materials.

Design Work

Design, to me, is the creation of a functional product through mostly artifial or digital means. What I mean by artificial is that it relies heavily on non-human tools, such as computers, large machines, printers, etc., to create something that serves a purpose other than just personal expression.

See mMre

Art Work

Art is a bit tricky to describe, but I think I nailed it with this one. Art is something created with no intention of actual use outside of being a form of expression, or a provocation of thought This can be primarily artificially made, physically made, or anywhere in between.

Craft Work

Creating something for the purpose of function is craft. Think of a table, or a desk, chairs, computer building, lamps. Anything that you can use and is primarily constructed physically is craft.
